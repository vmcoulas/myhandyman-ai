Most fastener problems are caused by **no pilot hole**, **wrong screw**, or **misalignment**.

## Pilot holes (when and why)

Use a pilot hole when:

- You’re close to an edge.
- The wood is hardwood.
- You need parts to pull tight without splitting.

Rule of thumb:

- Pilot diameter ≈ the screw’s **core** (shank without threads).

## Countersink and counterbore

- **Countersink**: makes the screw head sit flush.
- **Counterbore**: recesses the head below the surface (often used with plugs).

## Driving screws cleanly

- Use the correct bit (PH2, PZ2, T25, etc.).
- Apply firm pressure in line with the screw.
- For repeated driving, consider an **impact driver**.

## Preventing split wood

- Drill a pilot.
- Don’t overtighten.
- Keep at least ~**1"** from the end grain when possible.
