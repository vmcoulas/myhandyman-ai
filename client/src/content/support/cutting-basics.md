Cut quality comes from **support, blade choice, and a clear line**.

## Before you cut

- Verify your layout: see **[Measuring & Marking](/support/measuring-marking)**.
- Decide: **cut on the waste side** of the line.
- Support the work so the offcut can fall away without pinching the blade.

## Circular saw essentials

- Set blade depth so teeth extend ~**1/4" (6mm)** below the board.
- Use a **straightedge guide** for long cuts.
- Start the saw, then ease into the cut (don’t slam the blade into the wood).

## Miter saw essentials

- Hold the work tight to the fence.
- Let the blade reach full speed.
- Finish the cut, then keep the blade down briefly to avoid lifting splinters.

## Jigsaw essentials

- Use a fine-tooth blade for cleaner cuts.
- Keep the shoe flat on the work.
- For curves, go slower and avoid forcing the blade.

## Tear-out control

- Use a sharp blade.
- For plywood, use a blade designed for plywood/laminate.
- Masking tape over the cut line can help on some materials.
