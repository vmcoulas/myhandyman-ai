Accurate builds start with **repeatable measurement and clear marks**.

## Quick checklist

- Pick one **reference edge** (factory edge, straight edge, or one face) and measure from it every time.
- Mark with a **sharp pencil** or **knife line** for cleaner cuts.
- Use the same **units** (inches or mm) all the way through.
- Confirm the **"which side of the line"** rule before cutting.

## Tape measure tips

- **Hook play is normal.** The end hook moves slightly to account for inside/outside measurements.
- For short, critical dimensions, **burn an inch**:
  - Start at the 1" mark instead of the hook.
  - Measure to (target + 1"), then subtract 1".

## Marking straight lines

- For long lines, use a **straightedge** or **chalk line**.
- For short lines, a **combination square** is faster and more accurate.

## Common mistakes

- Measuring from different edges across parts.
- Reading the tape at an angle (parallax).
- Marking a line but cutting on the wrong side.
